## 0. Why this folder exists

Every folder from v16 on ended by pointing at the same missing piece. v16
found that latency equals logic depth and wrote, in plain text, that this is
"the structural reason a real brain needs a clock to stage arithmetic." v17
found the carry latency is linear in word width — a clock would have to wait
out that ripple. v18's continuous field *demanded* a capture latch to hold
transient results between stages. Three different builds, one recurring
conclusion: **the resonator neuron computes, but it needs something external to
say "now settle, now advance, now hold."** That something, in the brain, is the
theta rhythm, generated by the medial septum and paced onto the hippocampus.

Two papers describe exactly this: Nuñez & Buño 2021 (the biology — theta is a
septal pacemaker driving the hippocampus, phase-resetting on salient input,
nesting with gamma to organize computation into temporal sequences), and
Vardalakis et al. 2024 (the computational model — the septum as a bank of
coupled Kuramoto oscillators producing theta with phase reset). This folder
builds that pacemaker and uses it to drive the resonator neuron, replacing the
hand-coded `theta_gamma_clock()` the original adder only *assumed*.

The fit is not decorative. The septum is a **population of coupled oscillators
poised at the edge of synchrony, averaged into one rhythm** — structurally the
same mechanism as the Homeostatic Coupler at edge-of-chaos that produced the
**original PerceptionLab accident**. The arc began with an edge-poised
oscillator tipping into a rhythm; it closes here with the principled version of
that same pacemaker, now driving the neuron the accident inspired.

## 1. The pacemaker (Vardalakis 2024, faithfully)

`septum.py` implements the medial septum as N coupled Kuramoto phase
oscillators:

```
dtheta_i/dt = omega_i + (k/N) sum_j sin(theta_j - theta_i) + G_reset * X(t) * Z(theta_i)
```

`omega_i` are natural frequencies drawn Normal around 2*pi*f0 (theta center);
`k` is the coupling strength; the mean-field coupling reduces exactly to
`k*A*sin(phi - theta_i)` using the complex order parameter
`r = (1/N) sum exp(i theta_j) = A exp(i phi)`. The output theta drive is a
rectified cosine of the mean phase scaled by the coherence A — Vardalakis's
`I_theta(t)`. `X(t)` is the downstream (CA1) firing fed back to the septum, and
`Z(theta) = -sin(theta)` is the phase response curve (zero at the peak,
phase-advancing elsewhere, the hippocampal-pyramidal PRC form they cite from
Lengyel 2005 / Akam 2012).

Verified: from f0 = 7 Hz the bank self-organizes a 7.00 Hz rhythm with
coherence 0.82 — a theta clock that *emerges* from coupling, not one we impose.

## 2. Phase reset

`septum.py`'s second test delivers a brief pulse to one of two identical septa
and compares phases afterward. The pulse measurably resets the ensemble phase,
and the shift grows cleanly with the reset gain (≈1.5° at low gain up to ≈90°
at high gain). This is the mechanism both papers center on: a salient input
re-aligns the clock to a defined phase. It is also exactly what the v18
PerceptionLab graph already used without naming — the wire from the coupler's
regime into the neuron's reset ports. This folder characterizes that wire: it
is phase reset, and *when* in the cycle it lands determines its effect.

## 3. The loop closed: theta-paced arithmetic

`septum_paced_adder.py` replaces the hand-coded scheduler with the generated
theta clock. The septum self-organizes theta; each theta *peak* gates one
bit-stage of a ripple adder (the resonator gates settle within the cycle — the
gamma-fast work between theta peaks); the carry advances one stage per theta
cycle; and stage completion feeds back as the septum's reset input, closing the
loop.

```
5 + 3 on 4 bits, paced by generated theta:
  theta peak @ 0.048s : bit 0  a=1 b=1 -> sum=0 carry=1
  theta peak @ 0.180s : bit 1  a=0 b=1 -> sum=0 carry=1
  theta peak @ 0.312s : bit 2  a=1 b=0 -> sum=0 carry=1
  theta peak @ 0.449s : bit 3  a=0 b=0 -> sum=1 carry=0
  result: 5 + 3 = 8   (one carry hop per theta cycle, ~134 ms = 7.5 Hz)

random additions: 3-bit 12/12, 4-bit 8/8 correct
```

The addition runs on a clock the system *generates*, not one we impose, and the
computation resets that clock. `septum_paced.png` shows the rectified theta
humps with each carry hop landing on a theta peak, and the coherence dipping at
each feedback reset — the loop visibly live. This is the literal "theta-gamma
clock" the original `adder.py` modelled by assumption, now produced by the
pacemaker.

## 5. Honest limits

- The arithmetic here is at the *verified algebraic* resonator level (the gate
  truth tables proven exact in the repo core). The contribution of this folder
  is the **timing** — that the stage advance is driven by a generated,
  phase-resettable theta clock rather than a hand-coded loop — not a re-proof of
  the arithmetic, and not a full continuous-field run paced by theta (that would
  marry v18's field with v19's clock, the natural next build).
- The Kuramoto septum is the abstract pacemaker from Vardalakis, not a
  biophysical septum. It faithfully reproduces theta generation, coherence, and
  phase reset, but it is a phase-oscillator model, not conductance-based.
- The "same mechanism as the accident" claim is a **structural correspondence**
  (coupled edge-poised oscillators averaged into a rhythm), not an assertion
  that the homeostatic coupler and the Kuramoto bank are the identical system.
  Both sit at the edge and produce a rhythm; that is the shared form.
- Nothing here shows the brain computes arithmetic by resonator interference.
  What is shown is that the *timing scaffolding* the resonator results demanded
  is real, is generated by a separable pacemaker, works by phase reset, and can
  pace the computation with the loop closed.
- f0, k, and reset gain are model parameters; the 7 Hz theta and ~134 ms stage
  period are consequences of f0 = 7 Hz, not independently calibrated biology.
