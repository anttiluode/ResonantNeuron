PerceptionLab · solving by relaxing

The Sudoku that solves itself by cooling down

No search, no backtracking. Every row is kept a valid permutation, the grid is treated as a physical system whose energy counts column and box conflicts, and the solver swaps cells while a temperature slowly drops. The puzzle isn't searched for — it's annealed into existence as the lowest-energy state.

Pick a puzzle and press Anneal.
What you're watching. Cells glow hotter the more constraints they violate; the field cools from orange to blue as conflicts vanish. This is a faithful physical relaxation (simulated annealing on a conflict energy), the same "solve by relaxing" idea as Kuramoto/P-KAS oscillator solvers — but with the state space constrained so the relaxation actually converges instead of stalling. Hard puzzles may need several automatic restarts; that's the honest cost of relaxation, not a bug. Full explanation in the README.
